⚙️ Phase 3: Data Preprocessing

Goal: Clean anomalies, encode categories, and scale features for ML models.

🔧 Cholesterol Imputation
172 patients had Cholesterol = 0 (impossible clinically, 18.7% of data). Imputed using age-group median strategy instead of global median — preserving age-cholesterol relationship.
🏷️ One-Hot Encoding
Converted 5 categorical features (Sex, ChestPainType, RestingECG, ExerciseAngina, ST_Slope) into binary columns with drop_first=True to avoid multicollinearity.
📏 Feature Scaling
StandardScaler applied to numerical features (Age, RestingBP, Cholesterol, MaxHR, Oldpeak). Critical for SVM and KNN performance.
✂️ Train-Test Split
80/20 stratified split → 734 training, 184 testing. Stratification ensures class balance maintained in both sets.

⚠️ The Gap — Why Current Approaches Fail

In Low and Middle-Income Countries (LMICs), Acute Heart Failure accounts for 2.2% of total hospital admissions with a mean in-hospital mortality rate of 8%. Three critical gaps persist:

🔴 Late Detection
Patients in LMICs are younger but present at much more advanced clinical stages, reducing treatment effectiveness.
🟡 Underuse of Life-Saving Drugs
Only 57% of LMIC patients receive ACE inhibitors and only 32% receive MRAs — compared to >80% in high-income nations.
🟣 No Personalized Prioritization
Overloaded clinics (1 doctor per 100+ patients) lack tools to distinguish who needs urgent intervention vs. routine care.

Source: PLOS Medicine / PMC7162464

🎯 Our Solution — Counterfactual-Driven Risk Reduction

HeartGuard AI doesn't just predict risk — it simulates interventions using counterfactual reasoning to find the most impactful change for each patient.

📋
1. Patient Data Input
Collects 11 basic clinical features — BP, cholesterol, ECG, heart rate. No expensive labs or MRI/CT required.
🤖
2. ML Risk Prediction
Calculates individual heart disease probability (0–100%) using our 91% accurate Logistic Regression model.
🔮
3. Counterfactual Engine
"What-if" simulations: "If BP drops by 15 mmHg, risk falls to X%." Identifies the single most impactful change.
📊
4. Actionable Reporting
Generates a prioritized intervention list ranked by largest % risk drop. Doctors act on data, not guesswork.

🏥 Real-World Application

Designed for Rural Primary Health Centers (PHCs) and district clinics in settings like India, Haiti, and Sub-Saharan Africa. Heart failure prevalence in these settings is approximately 3.2%, driven by hypertension, obesity, poverty, and renal dysfunction.

Our simulator enables clinic staff to prioritize high-risk patients for intensive counseling or referral — replacing the "one-size-fits-all" approach with data-driven decisions. Weight reduction or BP titration with affordable generics can significantly drop 5-year HF risk for specific individuals.

Source: Haiti Cardiovascular Disease Cohort / AHA Journals
